# Antislop Rules

These rules apply to all human-facing prose generated by this system. Every piece of text stored in the database or shown in the dashboard must comply before it is saved.

## Em Dashes

Do not use em dashes (—). They create a pause that usually signals a lazy sentence structure. Rewrite the sentence, split it in two, or use a comma. The meaning should survive the surgery.

## Filler Openers

Never open a sentence or paragraph with these phrases:

- "In today's world"
- "It's worth noting"
- "As we can see"
- "At the end of the day"
- "In conclusion"
- "It goes without saying"
- "Needless to say"
- "The fact of the matter is"

These phrases add no information. Cut them. Start with the substance.

## Summarising Closers

Do not close a section or document with these phrases:

- "In summary"
- "To wrap up"
- "As we've seen"
- "In closing"

The reader just read the content. They do not need a verbal signal that it is ending. If a closing thought is warranted, state it directly.

## Hollow Intensifiers

The following adjectives are banned. They inflate the apparent importance of a claim without adding measurable meaning:

- "revolutionary"
- "game-changing"
- "unprecedented"
- "transformative"
- "cutting-edge"
- "innovative"
- "powerful"
- "robust"
- "seamless"
- "next-level"

If a claim deserves emphasis, back it with a number or a specific comparison. Otherwise, remove the intensifier and let the noun stand alone.

## Sentence Structure

Write short declarative sentences. One idea per sentence. When a sentence needs a conjunction to hold two ideas together, ask whether they are actually related. If they are not, split the sentence. If they are, make the relationship explicit rather than using "and" as a bridge.

## Evidence Before Claims

State the data point before drawing the conclusion. Do not open with the conclusion and then justify it. This order matters because it signals that the conclusion came from the data, not the other way around.

Wrong order: "This format is outperforming others, as shown by its 4.8% engagement rate."
Right order: "This format achieved a 4.8% engagement rate, the highest in the set. It is outperforming the others."

## Rhetorical Questions as Hooks

Do not open a piece of content with a rhetorical question. Questions used as hooks are a substitute for a strong first sentence. Write the strong first sentence instead.
